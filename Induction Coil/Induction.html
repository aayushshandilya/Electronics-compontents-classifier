Induction Coil

An induction coil or "spark coil" (archaically known as an inductorium or Ruhmkorff coil after Heinrich RÃ¼hmkorff) is a type of electrical transformer used to produce high-voltage pulses from a low-voltage direct current (DC) supply. To create the flux changes necessary to induce voltage in the secondary coil, the direct current in the primary coil is repeatedly interrupted by a vibrating mechanical contact called an interrupter.
